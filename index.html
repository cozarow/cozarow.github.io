hey! i'm cerulean ozarow, a rising senior studying applied math at brown university. aside from various math-adjacent things, my main research interests lie in symbolic systems (broadly writ), educational policy, and various aspects of religious studies, although i'm admittedly a little bit interested in nearly everything. in my free time, i like to read, write, make and listen to music, and go on adventures.

this past summer, i worked as a quantitative trading intern at jane street. i also have considerable experience with teaching — i've served as a TA at BEAM and for brown's discrete math course, and have tutored privately and through programs for underserved students in nyc and providence — and have conducted research in language policies in education with prof. pierre de galbert and language acquisition with the brown language and thought lab, among other projects. currently i'm the co-director of the logistics committee for NACLO, a linguistics competition for high school students in which i finished in the top 5 nationally in 2020. please feel free to contact me if you'd like to reach out about any opportunities or would like a full résumé!

this website is intended as a repository for various personal projects of mine. it's also been a way to learn how to hand-code a website! hope you enjoy :)
